# English Advanced — Format Guide

## HSC English Advanced Paper Structure

### Paper 1: Texts and Human Experiences (40 marks — 1hr 30min)
| Section | What | Marks | Time |
|---------|------|-------|------|
| Section I | Unseen texts — short answer questions | 20 | ~40 min |
| Section II | Essay — Prescribed text | 20 | ~50 min |

### Paper 2: Modules (60 marks — 2hr)
| Section | Module | Marks | Time |
|---------|--------|-------|------|
| Section I | Module A — Textual Conversations | 20 | ~40 min |
| Section II | Module B — Critical Study | 20 | ~40 min |
| Section III | Module C — Craft of Writing | 20 | ~40 min |

## Essay Grading Criteria (NESA bands)
| Band | Mark range | What it looks like |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Band 6 | 90-100 | Sophisticated analysis, seamless integration of quotes, complex thesis, strong personal voice |
| Band 5 | 80-89 | Clear thesis, good textual knowledge, mostly consistent analysis |
| Band 4 | 70-79 | Adequate — knows the text, basic analysis, some structure |
| Band 3 | 60-69 | Surface-level retelling of plot, minimal technique analysis |
| Band 2 | 50-59 | Describes without analysing, no coherent argument |

## Quick Checklist — Every Essay

### Before writing (2 min plan)
- [ ] Underline key words in question
- [ ] Brainstorm 3 ideas/themes
- [ ] Pick 2-3 quotes per paragraph
- [ ] Quick thesis statement

### During writing
- [ ] Integrate quotes — don't just plonk them in
- [ ] Technique + effect + link back to thesis
- [ ] Link each paragraph to the question
- [ ] Don't retell the plot

### After writing (if time)
- [ ] Check each paragraph has a clear topic sentence
- [ ] Does your conclusion answer "so what?"
- [ ] Spelling, grammar, punctuation

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Retelling the story | Assume the marker knows the text. Analyse, don't recount. |
| "Shakespeare uses..." without effect | Always ask: WHY did they use it? What does it make the reader feel/think? |
| Dropping quotes with no lead-in | Integrate: "When Portia declares that 'the quality of mercy is not strained', she..." |
| No thesis / Just describing | Everything should link back to your central argument |
| Forgetting context (Mod A) | The whole point is the conversation BETWEEN contexts |
| Not counting words | Aim for ~800-1000 per essay in timed conditions |
