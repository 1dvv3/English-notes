# "Elegy: The Absences" — Analysis

> **Collection:** David Malouf, *Earth Hour* (2014)

---

## Theme
Loss, memory, what remains after death; the presence of absence.

---

## What It's About

An elegy is a poem of mourning, but Malouf **inverts the form** — rather than listing what has been lost, he meditates on how ABSENCE itself has a kind of presence. The people and things we lose continue to shape us through their not-being-there.

---

## Key Idea For Your Essay
Malouf argues that absence is not nothing — it is a **negative space that defines the positive shape of our lives**. We are made as much by what we have lost as by what we have.

---

## Techniques to Spot

| Technique | What it creates |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Paradox** | "The presence of absence" — absence as a tangible thing |
| **Listing/litany** | Poem enumerates what is gone, the accumulation BECOMES the presence |
| **Conversational tone** | Malouf speaks as if to a friend — making vast ideas intimate |
| **Negation** | "Not this, not that" — defining by what is NOT there |

---

## Thesis Fragment

> *In "Elegy: The Absences," Malouf reframes loss not as diminishment but as a form of presence — the things we have lost shape us precisely through their absence, suggesting that identity is defined as much by what is gone as by what remains.*

---

## How It Links to the Collection

This poem is the collection's pivot from cosmic scale to intimate grief. Where "Earth Hour" meditates on humanity's place in the universe, "Elegy: The Absences" brings that meditation down to a single bereaved speaker. The technique of **enumeration** here echoes Malouf's method elsewhere — accumulation as a form of attention. What gets enumerated shifts (galaxies → lost people), but the structure persists, demonstrating textual integrity.
