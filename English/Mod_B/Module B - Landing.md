## The Big Picture

David Malouf (born 1934, Brisbane) — one of Australia's most celebrated writers. *Earth Hour* (2014) is a poetry collection meditating on:
- Time and mortality
- The natural world
- Art and memory
- What it means to be human in an age of ecological awareness

**"Earth Hour"** = both literal (global lights-off event for climate) AND metaphorical (pause, collective stillness, recognition of human smallness against geological time).

## Common Techniques Across the Collection
| Technique | What it does | Why Malouf uses it |
|-----------|-------------|--------------------|
| **Enjambment** | Lines run on without end-stops | Reflects continuous flow of time and consciousness — no clean breaks |
| **Free verse** | No fixed metre or rhyme | Mirrors unpredictability of experience; refuses artificial order |
| **Conversational tone** | As if speaking to a friend | Makes vast ideas (time, death, cosmos) feel intimate and accessible |
| **Nature imagery** | Trees, water, light, animals | The physical world is a LENS for the metaphysical |
| **Paradox** | "The presence of absence," "the silence that speaks" | Truth is not simple or singular |
| **Shift in scale** | Cosmic to intimate, galaxy to garden | Positions human as both insignificant (cosmically) AND meaningful (personally) |
| **Accumulation/lists** | Catalogues of what is lost, present | The sheer WEIGHT of things — accumulation as a form of attention |
