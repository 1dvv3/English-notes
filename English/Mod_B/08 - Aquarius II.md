# 8. Aquarius II — Analysis

> **Collection:** David Malouf, *Earth Hour* (2014)
> **Position:** Third-last poem of the original collection — closes the book like Aquarius opened it.

---

## What It's About

Aquarius II is the GRATEFUL counterpart to Aquarius. Where the opening poem was anxious-enchanted ("as if we shared / its gift and were immortal"), Aquarius II is at peace with finitude. Malouf expresses gratitude — for the beauty of the earth, for the body's good fortune, for the privilege of being mortal at all. The poem acknowledges the variety and fragility of life; what looked like threat in Aquarius is reframed as gift.

The zodiac name returns: Aquarius, water-bearer, bearer of everything that flows. But now the carrier is named as something for which thanks is owed.

---

## Key Ideas / Themes

| Theme | How it surfaces |
|-------|----------------|
| Gratitude for earthly things | Noticing breath, body, the ordinary — expressed as categorical thanks |
| Mortality accepted as gift | Denying death denies life. Acceptance opens the door to immortality of another kind |
| Variety + fragility as a single insight | Malouf sees the world's multiplicity and does not react with regret |
| Counterpoint to Aquarius | Bookends without symmetrical repetition — they are RHYMES, not mirror-images. |

---

## Key Techniques to Spot

| Technique | How it works |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Water imagery returning (Aquarius → Aquarius II)** | The water-bearer of the zodiac returns to the bearer of watery, mortal, contingent life |
| **Sentence-pace that resolves where Aquarius was anxious** | Shorter lines, more terminal pauses. The collection ends more peacefully than it opened |
| **Religious vocabulary repurposed casually** | "carry on" / "eternal" used in conversational register — secularised prayer |
| **Enumeration across life-forms** | Sky, water, animal, fellow-humans — the gratitude is catholic and specific |
| **Enjambment softer than Aquarius** | Aquarius II enjambs but with the rhythm of calm acceptance rather than the anxious flow of "weather" |

## How It Links to Other Poems

| Pair | Thematic linkage |
|------|------------------|
| **Aquarius II ↔ Aquarius** | Obvious pair. Two halves of one bookend: anxious-enchanted vs grateful-accepting. A reader can complete a thesis on the collection by reading just these two. |
| **Aquarius II ↔ Radiance** | Radiance catalogues wounds-as-gifts; Aquarius II is the THANK YOU issued once the catalogue is finished. The poems are successive movements — Radiance prepares Aquarius II. |
| **Aquarius II ↔ Towards Midnight (The Rapture)** | Both afterlife-themed in different registers. Rapture = rapture-of-the-dead at end-of-life. Aquarius II = gratitude-for-the-still-living-at-the-end-of-life. Rapture looks up; Aquarius II looks around. |
| **Aquarius II ↔ Earth Hour (title)** | Both poems about the privilege of being alive in this particular world. Earth Hour foregrounds politics + cosmos; Aquarius II focuses inward. They share the gesture but operate at different scales. |

## Thesis Fragment

> *Aquarius II closes the collection by giving the volume its quiet overall shape — gratitude for the gift of being mortal in a fragile, beautiful world. The poem refuses the Salmon-of-Wisdom-style consolations: nothing here says death is wrong, time is wrong, fragility is wrong. Malouf accepts them and thanks them. The bookend pairing with Aquarius completes the arc.*

---

## Sample Paragraph

> Read alongside Aquarius, Malouf's Aquarius II performs what textual integrity means in this collection: a thematic and structural rhyme across the volume. Where Aquarius opens in the anxious register of an immortal-seeming day — *"as if we shared / its gift and were immortal"* — Aquarius II closes with the explicit correction. To deny death is to deny the privilege of living. The technique that closes the book is not a new move: it is enjambment, it is free verse, it is the zodiac and water imagery of the opening poem — but the lines have a different tempo. Aquarius II does not spool out forward; it arrives. The grammatical texture changes. Where Aquarius was uncertain, Aquarius II is settled. Reading the pair as the volume's envelope, what emerges is Malouf's central claim about the meaning of *Earth Hour* as a whole: that the right relation to beauty and fragility is acceptance plus gratitude, not defence.
