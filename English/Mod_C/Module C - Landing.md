# Module C — The Craft of Writing

> **Module:** Craft of Writing (Paper 2, Section III — 20 marks, ~40 min)
> **Structure:** Part A (creative piece, ~25 min, 12 marks) + Part B (reflection, ~15 min, 8 marks)

---

## Exam Format
| Part | What | Marks | Words | Time |
|------|------|-------|-------|------|
| **Part A** | Imaginative, Discursive, Persuasive, OR Informative piece | 12 | ~600-800 | ~25 min |
| **Part B** | Reflection — justify your creative choices | 8 | ~300-400 | ~15 min |

---

## Question Types
| Type | What they ask | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Imaginative | Write a story | *"Compose a piece of imaginative writing that explores a moment of realisation."* |
| Discursive | Write a personal essay | *"Write a discursive piece that reflects on a significant relationship."* |
| Persuasive | Argue a position | *"Write a persuasive piece that argues for the importance of learning through failure."* |
| Informative | Explain / describe | *"Write an informative piece about a ritual or tradition."* |
| Reflection | Justify choices | *"Explain how your prescribed text(s) influenced your creative choices."* |

## Creative Piece Structure
1. **Opening** — hook the reader immediately (in medias res, striking image, question)
2. **Rising tension** — build conflict or emotional stakes
3. **Climax / Turning point** — moment of revelation or change
4. **Resolution** — not necessarily happy, but meaningful

**Key: SHOW, DON'T TELL. Use sensory details, dialogue, imagery.**

---

## Reflection Structure (4-part formula)
1. **Purpose** — what was I trying to achieve?
2. **Influences** — how did my prescribed text(s) inspire my choices? NAME the text, NAME a specific technique, EXPLAIN how you adapted it.
3. **Techniques** — what techniques did you use and why?
4. **Evaluation** — was I successful? What would you change?

---

## Timing Strategy — 40 Minutes
| Minute | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 0-2 | Read the question. Underline keywords. Decide: imaginative, discursive, or persuasive? |
| 2-5 | Quick plan: opening line, turning point, ending image. What prescribed text technique will you borrow? |
| 5-25 | **WRITE Part A** (the creative piece). Don't edit as you go — just get it down. |
| 25-28 | Quick read-over Part A. Fix glaring errors only. |
| 28-38 | **WRITE Part B** (the reflection). Use the four-part formula. Name-drop your prescribed text + specific technique. |
| 38-40 | Final read of both parts. Check for spelling/grammar. |
