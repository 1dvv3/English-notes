# Reflection Framework — Part B (Module C)

> Use this 4-part formula for every Part B response in craft of writing.

---

## The Formula

### 1. PURPOSE (1 sentence)
**Question:** What were you trying to achieve?

**Template:**
> *"I sought to explore [theme] by [form/genre choice]."*

**Examples:**
- *"I sought to explore how a single childhood moment can fundamentally alter one's understanding of the world — the shift from innocence to a more complex awareness."*
- *"I sought to dramatise the way grief reshapes a relationship by structuring the piece across two time zones."*
- *"I sought to use the conventions of the personal essay to examine [theme] through the lens of [specific experience]."*

---

### 2. INFLUENCES (2-3 sentences)
**Question:** How did your prescribed text(s) inspire your choices?

**CRITICAL RULES:**
- **NAME** the prescribed text (e.g., Gwen Harwood's "Father and Child")
- **NAME** the specific technique
- **EXPLAIN** how you adapted it to your context

**Template:**
> *"My prescribed text, [NAME], significantly influenced my choices. Specifically, [author]'s use of [technique] — as in '[brief quote]' — inspired me to [your adaptation]. I applied this by [concrete description of what you did]."*

**Example (Harwood-inspired):**
> *"My prescribed text, Gwen Harwood's 'Father and Child', significantly influenced my choices. Harwood's two-part structure — juxtaposing a childhood act of violence ('Barn Owl') with a reflective adult perspective forty years later ('Nightfall') — inspired me to include a temporal shift in my concluding paragraph."*

---

### 3. TECHNIQUES (2-3 sentences)
**Question:** What techniques did you use and WHY?

**CRITICAL RULES:**
- Name 2-3 specific techniques
- For each: what was the intended EFFECT on the reader?

**Template:**
> *"I employed [technique 1] to create [intended effect]; [technique 2] to [intended effect]; and [technique 3] to [intended effect]."*

**Example:**
> *"I employed 'in medias res' to create immediate immersion; concrete sensory imagery ('the knife, wrapped in a tea towel, the blade worn thin') to anchor abstract grief in the material world; and a single line of direct speech as a structural pivot — echoing Harwood's 'End what you have begun' with my own five-word command."*

---

### 4. EVALUATION (1-2 sentences)
**Question:** Were you successful? What would you change?

**CRITICAL RULES:**
- Be honest but positive
- Be SPECIFIC
- Mention what you would refine

**Template:**
> *"I believe my piece successfully [specific achievement], though I would refine [specific element] given more time / with further drafting."*

**Example:**
> *"I believe my piece successfully translates Harwood's approach to a contemporary, personal context while maintaining her emphasis on the way formative experiences shape our adult selves. Given more time, I would refine the pacing of the temporal shift in the final paragraph — the present-tense moment risks feeling rushed against the more developed childhood scene."*

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Vague influences ("I was inspired by Harwood") | Name the SPECIFIC technique + a brief quote |
| Listing techniques without effect | Always pair with the EFFECT on the reader |
| No evaluation / evaluation without specifics | State one concrete achievement AND one specific refinement |
| Mentioning the text but not engaging with it | Show that you UNDERSTOOD the technique — why does it work? |
| Repeating your Part A story in Part B | Part B is META — talk about the CRAFT, not the story |

---

## Sample Reflection (Model — Generic)

> My purpose in writing [piece title] was to [purpose]. I chose the [form] form to allow the reader to experience [what the form enables].
>
> My prescribed text, [AUTHOR]'s "[TITLE]", significantly influenced my choices. Specifically, [author]'s use of [technique with brief quote] inspired me to [your adaptation]. I applied this by [concrete description].
>
> I employed [technique 1] to [effect], [technique 2] to [effect], and [technique 3] to [effect]. Each technique was chosen because [brief reason linking to your purpose].
>
> I believe my piece successfully [specific achievement], though I would refine [specific refinement] with further drafting. Overall, the piece demonstrates my developing understanding of craft as conscious choice — every word a decision, every structure a stance.
